Reopening Berkeley

The slow return to normalcy amid a pandemic

What began as a year full of excitement, a new decade, quickly took a turn toward the unexpected. In late January, the spread of the coronavirus, also known as COVID-19 began in the United States. By late February, the first case of commuity transmitted coronavirus case was reported in the Bay Area. On March 17, Berkeley, along with the Bay Area, issued a shelter-in-place order.

The ramifications that followed were unpredictable; at the time, no one knew foresaw the chaos and unrest the virus would create. As many local business shut their doors, they remained hopeful that life would return to normal soon thereafter, but as we have come to know, it did not.

For what has seemed like an eternity—four months—people have remained at home and businesses closed. Slowly, though, the light at the end of this long tunnel has begun to shine. Cities have begun to proceed on their reopening plans, easing communities toward normalcy. Among these is Berkeley, who has allowed for small, local businesses to reopen with strict guidelines.
